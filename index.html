AP Bio is a marathon, not a sprint, and it’s basically the study of how life manages to stay organized despite the universe’s best efforts to turn everything into chaos (thanks, entropy). The curriculum is built around 4 Big Ideas. Here is a high-level "cheat sheet" thread to help you navigate the complexity. 🧬 Big Idea 1: Evolution The process of evolution drives the diversity and unity of life. It’s not just "monkeys to humans"—it’s about changes in genetic makeup over time. Natural Selection: Survival of the fittest (where "fitness" = reproductive success). Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium: A mathematical model used to calculate if a population is evolving. The Formulas: p+q=1 p 2 +2pq+q 2 =1 Conditions for equilibrium: Large population, no mutations, random mating, no gene flow, and no selection. Phylogeny: Using shared derived characters to build cladograms. ⚡ Big Idea 2: Energetics Biological systems use energy and molecular building blocks to grow, reproduce, and maintain dynamic homeostasis. Enzymes: Biological catalysts that lower activation energy. They are highly sensitive to pH and temperature. Cellular Respiration: Converting glucose into ATP. Key stages: Glycolysis, Krebs Cycle, and the Electron Transport Chain (ETC). Photosynthesis: The reverse—converting light energy into chemical energy. Light Reactions: Occur in the thylakoid; produce ATP and NADPH. Calvin Cycle: Occurs in the stroma; fixes CO 2 ​ into G3P (sugar). the relationship between photosynthesis and cellular respiration, AI generated Shutterstock 📑 Big Idea 3: Information Storage & Transfer Living systems store, retrieve, transmit, and respond to information essential to life processes. DNA to Protein: The Central Dogma. Transcription: DNA → RNA (in the nucleus). Translation: RNA → Protein (at the ribosome). Mendelian Genetics: Laws of Segregation and Independent Assortment. Gene Regulation: Operons (like the lac operon) in prokaryotes and transcription factors in eukaryotes. This is how a skin cell and a neuron have the same DNA but do totally different jobs. 🕸️ Big Idea 4: Systems Interactions Biological systems possess complex properties. This covers everything from the subcellular level to entire ecosystems. Cell Communication: Signal transduction pathways (Reception → Transduction → Response). Think of it like a molecular game of "Telephone." Ecology: Energy flow through trophic levels (the 10% rule) and population dynamics (exponential vs. logistic growth). Feedback Loops: * Negative Feedback: Maintains homeostasis (e.g., insulin/glucagon regulating blood sugar). Positive Feedback: Amplifies a process (e.g., oxytocin during childbirth or fruit ripening). 💡 Pro-Tip for the Exam The College Board loves to test your ability to analyze data. Don’t just memorize the steps of the Krebs cycle; understand what happens to the organism if one enzyme in that cycle is inhibited by a toxin.
